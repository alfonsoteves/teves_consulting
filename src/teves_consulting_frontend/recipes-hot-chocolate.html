Whole Foods Recipes: Hot Chocolate

Last updated: February 27, 2026

A clean hot chocolate is just real chocolate, warm milk, and a small amount of sugar—simple and reliably better than mixes.

Key takeaways
  • Real chocolate — melt high-cocoa chocolate into milk for a deeper flavor.
  • Gentle heat — avoid boiling to keep texture smooth.
  • Small sugar — adjust slowly; 90% chocolate needs less than you think.

Purpose

Create a fast, repeatable hot chocolate using whole ingredients and simple technique.

Total time

5–7 minutes

Ingredients (1 large mug)

  • Whole milk — 1½ cups
  • 90% dark chocolate — 20–30g (about 2 squares), finely chopped
  • Sugar — 1–2 teaspoons, adjust to taste
  • Sea salt (optional) — a tiny pinch
  • Vanilla extract (optional) — a small splash

Keep it simple. The quality of the chocolate does most of the work.

Method

1) Warm the milk

Heat milk over medium-low until steaming. Avoid boiling.

2) Melt the chocolate

Add chopped chocolate and whisk gently until fully melted and smooth.

3) Sweeten and finish

Add sugar, stir to dissolve, then taste. Optional: add a tiny pinch of salt to make the chocolate flavor pop.

4) Serve

Serve immediately while hot and smooth.

Notes

  • Adjust sweetness slowly — 90% chocolate can be intense; small sugar additions go a long way.
  • Lighter version — add a bit more milk and slightly less chocolate.
  • Best fresh — this is best enjoyed right away.
