Burger Buns (Soft / Brioche Direction)

Soft, slightly rich homemade buns designed for consistency, structure, and kid-friendly texture.

Overview

These buns build on a classic enriched dough with a slight shift toward a brioche-style softness. The goal is a bun that is tender, lightly sweet, and strong enough to hold fillings without falling apart.

Ingredients

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1 egg
  • 4 tbsp butter (softened)
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 2 ¼ tsp yeast (1 packet)

Process

1. Mix

Combine warm milk, yeast, and sugar. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until slightly foamy. Add egg, butter, flour, and salt. Mix until dough forms.

2. Knead

Knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be soft, slightly enriched, and not sticky.

3. First Rise

Cover and let rise for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

4. Shape

Divide into equal portions (6–8 buns). Shape into tight, smooth balls by creating surface tension. Place on baking sheet with space between them.

5. Second Rise

Let rise again for 45–60 minutes until puffy.

6. Egg Wash

Brush tops lightly with beaten egg for color and shine.

7. Bake

Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15–18 minutes until golden brown.

Key Principles

  • Enriched dough: Milk, butter, egg, and sugar create softness and flavor
  • Surface tension: Proper shaping creates structure and appearance
  • Do not overbake: Keeps interior soft and tender

Notes

These buns are ideal for burgers, sandwiches, and breakfast. The slight increase in butter and sugar improves softness and makes them more appealing to children. They can be stored for a couple of days or frozen for later use.

Philosophy

Simple ingredients, controlled process, and repeatable results. Small adjustments can significantly improve texture and enjoyment.
